Branching Narratives:

Video Games' Evolution of Storytelling

Player Choices in Action

In the video game Until Dawn, players are confronted with a pivotal moment that demonstrates the essence of interactive storytelling. One of the defining choices of the game occurs when the player is confronted with a lever. Pulling it one way results in the demise of Ashley, while the other way results in the killing of Josh.

The player's decision at this juncture not only determines the immediate fate of the characters but also sends ripples through the storyline, unveiling the complexity inherent in video game narratives. Opting to save Ashley, Josh appears to be graphically killed by the saw; however, it is later revealed that Josh had planned the encounter, and his body was a false body. Opting to save Josh, the saw approaches Ashley but stops just in time, leaving Ashley alive. In both situations, there are possibilities of them both being killed in the future.

As players grapple with decisions like choosing between Ashley and Josh, they become unwitting architects of the unfolding narrative. The impact of these choices extends beyond immediate outcomes, creating a dynamic and branching storyline where every decision adds a layer of complexity. This ripple effect challenges the linear structure of cinematic storytelling, introducing unpredictability and replayability that keeps them engaged.

Additionally, the collaborative nature of video game storytelling is accentuated in the design of these narrative experiences. Game designers craft decision points, character arcs, and plot twists, laying the foundation and basis for a compelling story. Here, the collaborative venture unfolds, as the creators design a scenario where the choices appear to lead to irrevocable consequences, fostering a sense of agency for players and bringing weight to every decision. However, behind the scenes, the narrative is designed to pivot and reveal the true nature of these consequences, creating a dynamic and unexpected turn of events.

In this manipulation of player perception, the collaborative nature of game storytelling is revealed. The creators set the stage for players to contribute to the narrative not only through their choices but also through their emotional reactions and interpretations. Players believe they are shaping the story in one direction, only to later realize the narrative has been orchestrated in another way.

These collaborative deceptions and pivotal player decisions challenge the conventional boundaries of cinematic storytelling and transform the player from a passive participant into an active contributor to the narrative. The collaboration between creator and player in Until Dawn goes both ways, with the creators crafting a narrative that toys with expectations, and in turn, players contribute to the story through their emotional investment and interpretations.

Throughout many different video game narratives, this collaborative interaction between creators and players emerges as a crucial part of the medium. It highlights the malleability of storytelling in video games, where the narrative can twist and turn in unexpected directions, revealing a shared journey where both creators and players actively shape the narrative experience.
